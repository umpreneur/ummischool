Purpose:
A calm, editorial-style website about homeschooling (thuisonderwijs), written in Dutch.
It’s designed for parents who are searching, doubting, or already homeschooling — a place of clarity, calm, and connection.

The site should feel like a quiet online magazine:
warm, simple, text-focused, and visually balanced.

🧭 Main Navigation (Dutch menu names)

Wat & Waarom

Informative, scrollable page (not a blog).

Divided into sections explaining:

What homeschooling is

Why families choose it

Common misunderstandings

Basic legal or practical context

A sidebar or sticky navigation allows users to jump between sections easily.

Smooth scroll transitions and subtle dividers for readability.

Beginnen

Practical guide page (scrollable, not a blog).

Sections such as:

First steps

Daily rhythm

Structuring the day

Materials and resources

How to combine homeschooling with family life

Sidebar navigation for easy access to sections.

Blog

Dynamic content section for articles and printables.

Contains subcategories such as:

Ervaringen (experiences)

Thema’s / Seizoenen

Printables

Opvoeding & balans

Archive section where users can browse older posts or filter by topic.

Editorial layout: large headings, white background, clear typography.

Contact

Simple, standard form with: name, email, message.

Small text introducing the purpose of contact (questions, collaborations, consulting).

Minimal design, aligned with the rest of the site.

🎨 Design & Style

Visual tone: calm, real, warm, minimal.

Inspiration: The Gentlewoman, Kinfolk, Cerealmag.

Typography:

Headlines: Cormorant Garamond or Playfair Display (serif)

Body text: Inter or Manrope (sans-serif)

Color palette:

Primary text: soft black (#1E1E1E)

Accent: warm beige (#EDE9E3) or muted olive (#B6B29E)

Background: off-white (#FAF9F7)

Layout:

Wide margins, centered text blocks

Subtle divider lines between sections

No icons or bright colors — purely editorial tone

Header: minimalist navbar (transparent, becomes solid on scroll)

Footer: clean with small tagline (“over rust, richting & groei”)

🧱 Technical notes

Fully responsive for mobile, tablet, and desktop.

Blog content managed via markdown or a simple CMS.

“Wat & Waarom” and “Beginnen” pages are long scrollable sections (with anchors in sidebar).

“Blog” includes archive filter by topic/date.

“Contact” uses minimal HTML form (no CAPTCHA).

Light mode only.

🪶 Summary

Thuisonderwijs should feel like a modern, quiet online magazine —
minimalist, text-driven, focused on warmth and real experience,
not on flashy design or heavy visuals.
